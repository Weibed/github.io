Fire Engine

--Truck Features--

Outboard Sensors
- detects multi-angle impacts allowing for faster deployment of aribags with sensor mounted on the outer perimeter of the cab.
Advanced Seatbelts
- Pulls the occupant into the seat in the event of an impact or rollover.
Vibra Torq Body-Mount System
- Reduces vibration and stresses on the body creating a smoother ride.
Standard Equipment
- Most engines will have a standard tool set which includes ladders, hydraulic rescue tools a.k.a jaws of life, firehose, SBA's, axe's and halligan bars.

What Does A Fire Department Use An Engine For?

For most emergencies the fire engine is the first truck that will be leaving the firestation. The engine has all the equipment necessary to go to a structure fire or a personal injury incident. The primary purpose of this vehicle is to be able to safely transport fire fighters to the scene of a incident with water and equipment to tackle any task.
