Spray Foam Insulation vs. Fiberglass Batt Insulation

Spray foam insulation is one of most advanced insulating materials available to the construction industry. The insulation is spray applied using a combination of chemicals and when properly installed, forms an air seal that prevents leaks, minimizes noise, and improves building performance. Fiberglass batt insulation has been used for many years as a cost effective way to insulate your home. This insulation is pressed lightly into wall cavities to slow down heat transfer throughout your building.

Spray Foam Insulation

Spray Foam also known as a polyurethane foam can be used in a wide variety of applications. It is capable of withstanding direct and prolonged contact with water without sustaining significant damage and is classified by fema as a flood resistant insulation material. This makes spray foam a great choice when insulating foundation walls in basements and crawl spaces.
Spray foam is the perfect thermal and moisture management system for your building project. Air moves in and out of a home through every hole, crack and seam. Approximately one third of air infiltrates through ceiling, walls and floor openings. Spray foam insulation is an important component in a moisture management system. Air infiltration and exfiltration, within a structure, contributes to approximately 99% of moisture migration. Reducing air movement deters moisture accumulation so that mold growth is less likely to occur. By controling moisture levels the growth of mildew and allergens can be minimized.

Fiberglass Batt Insulation

Fiberglass batts which consists of extremely fine glass fibers woven together is the insulation that is used in most homes today. It can be placed in walls, floors and even ceilings. In different areas of the country where climate changes from warm to cold throughout the seasons or in some instances even the days, it is required to have a vapor barrier installed along with the fiberglass batts. This vapor barrier is usually a 4mm to 6mm plastic wrap also known as poly.
Fiberglass as an insulator slows the spread of heat, cold, and sound in structures.The material does this by trapping pockets of air, keeping rooms warm in the winter and cooler in the summer.Fiberglass batts are cheap to produce resulting in a more affordable solution for building insulation. The downside of fiberglass batts is that if exposed to wind and moisture the fiberglass batt becomes inefficient and in most cases is no longer providing insulation as its intended use.
